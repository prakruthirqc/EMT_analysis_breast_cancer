### **Transcriptomic Analysis of TGF-β Signaling and EMT in Breast Cancer**



**Project Overview**



This project analyzes the relationship between TGF-β signaling and epithelial–mesenchymal transition (EMT) in breast cancer cell lines using transcriptomic data.



**Dataset**



Gene expression and phenotype data were obtained from the UCSC Xena database (Heiser 2012 breast cancer cell line dataset).



**Genes Analyzed**



* &#x20;TGF-β signaling: TGFB1, TGFBR1, SMAD2, SMAD3, SMAD4
* &#x20;EMT markers: CDH1, VIM
* &#x20;EMT transcription factors: SNAI1, TWIST1, ZEB1
* &#x20;Proliferation marker: MKI67
* &#x20;Apoptosis marker: BCL2



&#x20;**Methods**



1\. Data preprocessing and gene extraction

2\. Subtype-based gene expression analysis (Basal vs Luminal)

3\. Boxplot visualization

4\. EMT heatmap analysis

5\. Gene correlation analysis

6\. Differential expression analysis

7\. Volcano plot visualization



&#x20;**Results**



* &#x20;The basal subtype showed higher expression of TGF-β signaling genes and EMT markers.
* &#x20;Luminal subtype showed higher expression of epithelial marker CDH1.
* &#x20;Heatmap analysis helped to differentiate Basal (a more aggressive type of cancer) and luminal cell line samples and differential gene expression associated with them.
* &#x20;TGF-β signaling genes positively correlated with EMT transcription factors in the correlation heatmap.
* &#x20;Volcano plot showed upregulation of EMT markers in the Basal subtype.



&#x20;**Conclusion**



The analysis suggests that TGF-β signaling is associated with EMT activation in Basal breast cancer cell lines, which may contribute to aggressive and metastatic phenotype.
